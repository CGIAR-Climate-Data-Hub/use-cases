## Brief

### Background & rationale

iCLEANED is a decision-support tool designed to identify potential environmental benefits and risks of livestock interventions. It is suited for data-scarce regions requiring rapid, evidence-based decisions. Using a minimum set of input data, the tool estimates the environmental footprint of livestock systems — including land use, water use, soil health (nutrient balance and soil erosion), and greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions. It also estimates livestock productivity and economic value through a cost–benefit component.

The tool is designed primarily for ex-ante analysis: users can compare scenarios (e.g. improving animal feed, changing management practices, transitioning production systems) before implementing an intervention. iCLEANED integrates participatory assessment — bringing together farmers, experts, and stakeholders — with biophysical modelling.

How the CDH can support iCLEANED is to be explored in an upcoming call.
